⏰ Verb tenses

Present Simple

Use: habits, routines, general truths.

Form: I/You/We/They play · He/She/It plays.

Markers: always, usually, often, sometimes, never, every day.

Present Continuous

Use: actions happening now / temporary actions / future plans.

Form: am/is/are + verb-ing.

Markers: now, at the moment, today, this week, currently.

Past Simple

Use: finished actions in the past.

Form: regular: verb + -ed; irregular: 2ª columna (went, saw, did, took…).

Markers: yesterday, last week, in 2010, ago, when I was younger.

Past Continuous

Use: action in progress in the past / interrupted by another action.

Form: was/were + verb-ing.

Markers: while, as, when (combined with past simple).

I was studying when she called me.

Present Perfect

Use: past action with present relevance / experience / unfinished time period.

Form: have/has + past participle (3ª columna).

Markers: ever, never, just, already, yet, since, for, this week, today.

I have lived in Madrid for 5 years (still living there).

Past Perfect

Use: action that happened BEFORE another past action.

Form: had + past participle.

When I arrived, the train had already left.

Future — will

Use: spontaneous decisions, predictions without evidence, promises, offers.

I'll help you with your homework.

Future — going to

Use: plans/intentions decided before / predictions based on evidence.

Look at those clouds! It's going to rain.

🛡️ Modal verbs

ModalUseExample
can / can'tAbility, permissionI can swim. Can I leave?
could / couldn'tPast ability, polite requestI couldn't sleep. Could you help me?
must / mustn'tStrong obligation; prohibitionYou must study. You mustn't smoke.
have to / don't have toObligation (external); not necessaryI have to wear a uniform.
should / shouldn'tAdviceYou should go to bed early.
may / mightPossibilityIt might rain tomorrow.
wouldPolite request, hypotheticalWould you like a coffee?
⚠️ Difference must vs. have to:
  • You must finish — sense of personal/internal obligation.
  • You have to wear a uniform — external rule.
Mustn't ≠ don't have to:
  • You mustn't smoke = it's prohibited.
  • You don't have to go = it's not necessary.

Rules — modal verbs

  • Modals NEVER take "-s" in 3rd person: He can swim (NOT cans).
  • After a modal use the infinitive without to: You should go (NOT to go).
  • Negation: modal + n't (or not). I cannot / can't / mustn't / shouldn't.
  • Question: modal first. Can you help me?

🔀 Conditionals

Zero conditional — General truths

If + present simple, present simple.

If you heat water to 100°C, it boils.

First conditional — Real future possibility

If + present simple, will + infinitive.

If it rains tomorrow, I will stay at home.

Variants: instead of "will" we can use modals: If you study, you can pass.

Second conditional — Hypothetical / unreal present

If + past simple, would + infinitive.

If I had a million euros, I would travel the world.

With "be" we use were for all persons (formal): If I were you, I would tell the truth.

💡 Comparison:
  • 0: If you don't eat, you get hungry. (always true)
  • 1: If you don't eat, you will get hungry. (likely future)
  • 2: If you didn't eat, you would get hungry. (hypothetical)

🔄 Passive voice

Form

Subject + to be (correct tense) + past participle (+ by + agent).

TenseActivePassive
Present S.They make cars.Cars are made.
Past S.They built the house.The house was built.
Pres. Perf.They have finished it.It has been finished.
WillThey will fix it.It will be fixed.
ModalThey can solve it.It can be solved.

How to transform from active to passive (3 steps)

  1. Find the OBJECT of the active sentence → it becomes the new SUBJECT.
  2. Conjugate "to be" in the SAME TENSE as the active verb + past participle of the main verb.
  3. The original SUBJECT becomes the agent (preceded by "by"), or is omitted if not important.
Active: Shakespeare wrote Hamlet.
→ Object: Hamlet (new subject).
→ Active verb: wrote (past simple) → was + written.
→ Subject becomes "by Shakespeare".
Passive: Hamlet was written by Shakespeare.
💡 Tip: we use the passive when the action is more important than the doer, or when the doer is unknown.

Statements

say / tell + (that) + clause. (Tell is followed by an indirect object: tell me, tell him.)

"I am tired" → He said (that) he was tired.
"I love pizza" → She told me (that) she loved pizza.

Questions

Use asked + if/whether (yes/no) or asked + question word (wh-). Word order is affirmative.

"Do you speak French?" → He asked if I spoke French.
"Where do you live?" → She asked where I lived.

Commands

Use told + person + to/not to + infinitive.

"Open the door" → She told me to open the door.
"Don't be late" → He told us not to be late.

📖 Reading practice

Reading 1 — Teen life
Sarah is a 15-year-old girl from Brighton. Every morning, she wakes up at 7 and has breakfast with her family. She is studying very hard this term because she has important exams in May. Last weekend, she went to London with her friends and they visited the British Museum. Sarah has been there twice this year. Next summer, she is going to travel to Spain to improve her Spanish. If she passes all her exams, her parents will buy her a new phone.

Questions:

  1. How old is Sarah and where does she live?
  2. Why is she studying so hard now?
  3. What did she do last weekend?
  4. How many times has she been to the British Museum this year?
  5. What's her plan for next summer?
  6. What will happen if she passes all her exams?
  1. She is 15 years old and lives in Brighton.
  2. Because she has important exams in May.
  3. She went to London with her friends and visited the British Museum.
  4. Twice (= two times).
  5. She is going to travel to Spain to improve her Spanish.
  6. Her parents will buy her a new phone.
Reading 2 — Climate change
Climate change is one of the biggest problems of our time. Scientists have warned us for decades that human activity is causing the planet to warm up. If we continue burning fossil fuels, sea levels will rise and many cities will be flooded. We should use renewable energy and must reduce plastic waste. In some countries, plastic bags have been banned. Young people like Greta Thunberg are fighting to make governments take action.

Questions:

  1. What is the text mainly about?
  2. What will happen if we continue burning fossil fuels?
  3. What two things should/must we do, according to the text?
  4. What has been banned in some countries?
  5. Why is Greta Thunberg mentioned?
  1. Climate change and what we should do about it.
  2. Sea levels will rise and many cities will be flooded.
  3. Use renewable energy and reduce plastic waste.
  4. Plastic bags have been banned (in some countries).
  5. As an example of a young person fighting to make governments take action.

✍️ Writing — How to score points

Structure of a short essay (80-120 words)

  1. Introduction (1-2 sentences): present the topic.
  2. Body (3-4 sentences): develop ideas with examples and connectors.
  3. Conclusion (1-2 sentences): wrap up your opinion.

Useful connectors

FunctionConnectors
Adding ideasalso, in addition, moreover, furthermore, besides
Contrastingbut, however, although, on the other hand, nevertheless
Causebecause, since, as
Effectso, therefore, as a result, consequently
Examplesfor example, for instance, such as
Sequencefirst(ly), then, next, after that, finally
Conclusionin conclusion, to sum up, all in all, in short

Useful opinion phrases

  • In my opinion, …
  • I think / I believe that …
  • It seems to me that …
  • From my point of view, …
  • I (don't) agree with the idea that …

Sample essay topic — "Should teenagers use social media?"

Nowadays, social media is part of teenagers' daily lives. In my opinion, social media has both advantages and disadvantages.

On the one hand, platforms like Instagram or TikTok help young people communicate with friends and learn about the world. For example, many students use YouTube to study. Moreover, social media can be useful to develop creativity.

On the other hand, spending too much time online can be harmful. Many teenagers feel anxious because of comparisons with others, and they sleep less because they use their phones at night.

To sum up, I believe social media is positive if we use it sensibly, but parents and schools should help teenagers find a healthy balance.
